Mohamed El Alami

About

I used to be a mathematician. I completed a PhD in Mathematics at Stony Brook University, under the supervision of Mark McLean. I also did (part of) a postdoc at the University of Edinburgh under the supervision of Nick Sheridan. My research interests lied somewhere between symplectic and algebraic geometry. More specifically, I was interested in the applications of holomorphic curve theory to the study of symplectic topology and mirror symmetry.

Academic Work

  • Maurer--Cartan elements in symplectic cohomology from compactifications, with Matthew Strom Borman and Nick Sheridan.
  • An \(L_{\infty}\) structure on symplectic cohomology, with Matthew Strom Borman and Nick Sheridan.
  • An open GW-formula for Lagrangians in Fano varieties.
  • SYZ for index 1 Fano hypersurfaces in projective space, based on my PhD thesis.
